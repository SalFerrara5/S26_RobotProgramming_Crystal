Looking back at the “Assessing” and “Expanding Background Knowledge” sections, I realized how much frames and coordinate systems matter in robotics. The assessing part reminded me that plotting points isn’t just about marking spots, it’s about understanding how frames relate to each other. Figuring out the position of one frame relative to another showed that moving along axes isn’t always as straightforward as it seems, and using vector notation makes it way clearer.

The expanding section took it further with rotations and the right-hand rule in 3D. It made me see why having a consistent convention for axes and rotation directions is so important, especially when working with URDFs or programming joints. I liked how it connected back to the URDF stuff, the position and orientation of joints really determine how child links move relative to parent links. Also, realizing that rotating then translating isn’t the same as translating then rotating was a solid reminder that the order of operations actually matters in 3D. Bottom line: understanding frames and rotations isn’t just theory, it’s essential for making robot models that actually work.
